VCT STAGE 2 - CHALLENGERS EMEA WEEK 1: ACEND AND TEAM LIQUID BACK ON TOP


Following a relatively disappointing VCT Stage 1 Masters for EMEA, the squads are back with a vengeance coming into Stage 2. With Week 1 seeing familiar faces like Fnatic and G2 Esports skyrocket to the top of the leaderboard, relative dark horses BBL and current champions, Acend, have also dominated Stage 2's inaugural weekend.

Miss any of the action? Or simply looking to relive it? Here's our breakdown of VCT Stage 2 Challengers Week 1.



Group A: The perfect start for Acend, Fnatic and G2



Following a disappointing Stage 1 Challengers for resident Champions, Acend, the squad are back in black (or white and blue) and ready to rumble. The return of star player Aleksander "zeek" Zygmunt after a short hiatus has breathed new life into the team, marking them as ones to watch all over again.

Fnatic have also come back with a point to prove following their disappointing Stage 1 Masters run. With new player Emir Ali "Alfajer" Beder guiding the team to a hard-fought victory over FPX, his Raze is set to blast competitors away if left unchecked.

Fellow esports behemoths, G2 Esports, also came out of the gate swinging. Taking on DACH Regional League champions, FOKUS, the squad gave Future Earth’s resident samurai a run for their money, but unfortunately fell at the final hurdle on Icebox, giving G2 the win.



Group B: BBL, Guild and Team Liquid on top



Turkey’s finest, BBL, have come into Stage 2 with victory in their sights, taking down esports icons NAVI in a reverse sweep that saw 34 matches played on Icebox alone. While next week may see them stumble against the might of Team Liquid, this win is the perfect way to start off their run.

Liquid won’t go down easy, though, as the squad easily felled Berlin International Gaming across Icebox and Split in a series that, unfortunately, wasn’t BIG’s best. While they’re set to meet their match in the form of BBL, it’ll be interesting to see if the Turkist titans manage to topple one of Europe’s most iconic orgs.

Matching their tempo is Guild, who clashed with M3C in a battle for the ages. With Leo "Leo" Jannesson flexing on the competition in style, Britain’s finest find themselves at the top of the leaderboard.




WEEK 1 RECAP - ALL OF THE ACTION FROM EMEA’S REGIONAL LEAGUES


It’s VCT Stage 2, and the Regional Leagues are back in business. With some of the best teams from across EMEA duking it out to take home their region’s crown, the stakes are as high as ever

For those who need a quick refresher, EMEA is split into seven different regions: DACH, East, France, MENA, Northern Europe, Spain, and Turkey. This time around, each Regional League will have its own Grand Final, where one team will emerge victorious.



While we’re only in Week 1, we’ve already seen some insane series, with a whole swathe of teams coming out of the starting gate swinging. Miss any of the action? Here’s our full breakdown of what happened.



VALORANT Regional League DACH: Evolution



Having narrowly missed out on taking home the title during Stage 1, DSYRE are back with victory in their sights. A dominant showing across Icebox and Haven propelled them into the lead alongside fellow behemoths, MOUZ and Ovation Esports.

Angry Titans and ALTERNATE aTTaX also put on quite the show during their back and forth opening series. Battling it out across Split and Ascent, each team walked away with a map victory and will be looking to continue to add more wins to their belt in Week 2.

VALORANT Regional League East: Surge



Following their disappointing playoffs run during Stage 1, NOM Esports have returned to the fray with fire in their hearts and victory in their minds. Making Bind their stomping ground in each of their matches, they buried their enemies beneath the dusty plains of the abandoned Middle Eastern hamlet.

They’ll have to be aware of a rival squad, Diamant Esports, though. Slovenia’s finest had a dominant showing against both Zero Tenacity and Entropiq, propelling themselves to the top of the leaderboard.



VALORANT Regional League France: Revolution



Once again France: Revolution is a star-studded Regional League, packed with some of esports’ most famous faces. While it’s no surprise to see seasoned veterans beGenius at the top of the scoreboard, Karmine Corp have made quite the entry to VALORANT.

Only dropping six games across both matches, they demolished both Sector One and Mandatory, with the latter only taking a single round. With Amine "Amilwa" Saidi bending Sova to his will and starting off fights with a bang, the sky's the limit for the newcomers.



VALORANT Regional League MENA: Resilience



MENA’s Regional League is split into two different sections: GCC and Iraq, and Levant and North Africa.

GCC and Iraq

Will anyone ever topple Team Falcons? After a dominant run in Stage 1, Saudia Arabia’s finest have soared to the top of the table once more - but it hasn’t been easy. Dropping a game in three of their series, the competition is high and the stakes even higher for the veteran squad.

Hot on their heels are YaLLa Esports, who picked up former CS pro turned VALORANT star, Issa "ISSAA" Murad in the off-season. With an extensive history playing on top-tier teams like OG and Hellraisers, his debut week saw him play everything from Jett to Chamber marking him not only as one to watch, but a flexible player with a seemingly boundless skillset.



VALORANT Regional League Northern Europe: Polaris



Coming into Stage 2 with a reformed roster, EXCEL have already made their mark on Northern Europe: Polaris. Sweeping Human Tripwires (who also have a snazzy new logo) aside, they sit alongside Finest, KOVA, and who cars? at the top of the standings.

Who cars? also took down the powerhouse of Alliance across Bind and Split, already adding an early upset to what promises to be a region absolutely full of them.

VCT EMEA STAGE 2 CHALLENGERS BEGINS MAY 13



Everything you need to know about the next stage of Europe’s premier VALORANT competition



Welcome back to VCT EMEA! Stage 2 picks up where Stage 1 left off — 12 of EMEA’s best VALORANT teams battling for regional supremacy, Circuit Points and a handful of coveted international invitations.

In Stage 1, few teams could keep up with Fnatic and FunPlus Phoenix, group winners who soon found themselves pitted against each other in the Playoffs’ Upper Bracket Final. FPX won that match — featuring a magnificent 13-0 performance on Split — en route to winning the Grand Final 3-1 against G2 Esports, but their victory was bittersweet. The Stage 1 Champions could not compete at Masters 1 due to travel restrictions, and no EMEA team made Top 4 at Reykjavik.

With plenty left to prove, can FPX remain Kings of EMEA, or will a new team unseat them at the top? In order to follow all the action, here’s everything you need to know about VCT EMEA Stage 2 Challengers.



FORMAT OVERVIEW



Like Stage 1, the VCT EMEA Stage 2 Challengers format is divided into the Group Stage and Playoffs. During the Group Stage, 12 teams are drawn into two groups of six, where each group plays a single Round Robin. The top three teams of each group advance to the Playoffs, with the first place teams granted an opening bye. All Group Stage matches are best-of-three.

For Playoffs, the six qualified teams compete in a double elimination bracket. All matches are best-of-three, except for the Lower Bracket Final and Grand Final, which are best-of-five. The top three Playoff finishers will represent EMEA at Masters 2.

All VCT EMEA Stage 2 Challengers teams will receive Circuit Points based on their finish, points that are needed to qualify for VCT Champions or the Last Chance Qualifier.

PARTICIPATING TEAMS



To determine the groups for VCT EMEA Stage 2 Challengers, the 12 teams were separated into two pools — Stage 1 playoff teams (Pool 1) and Stage 1 teams that fell in the Stage 1 Group Stage (Pool 2). The newly promoted team FOKUS replaced relegated side SuperMassive Blaze in Pool 2.

Teams were randomly drawn from one pool at a time, starting with Pool 1, followed by Pool 2. Drawn teams were placed into groups in alternating order until the groups were filled.
